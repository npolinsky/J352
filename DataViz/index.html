How Rule Changes Have Shaped The Kickoff

Nick Polinsky | April 19, 2026

Just two years ago, the kickoff, once electrifed by the return presence of guys like Devin Hester, Jacoby Jones, and Cordarelle Patterson was a dying play. In 2023, the NFL saw an all-time low return rate of just 22.9%. Touchbacks brought the football out to the 25-yard line, and teams were okay with booting it out of the endzone rather than risking a chunk return.

After the 2023 NFL season, the league implimented its new dynamic kickoff, placing the kickoff coverage team stationary at the 40-yard line, and allowing them to release once the ball is caught. The touchback spot became the 30-yard line for all touchbacks in the endzone, and the 20-yard line for any ball that landed in the new designated "landing zone" and was then downed for a touchback. There were some immediate statistical changes, but still no major jumps. The average return distance jumped from 23.0 yards in 2023 to 27.6 yards in 2024, but the return rate was still only 34.7%

Fast forward to the offseason, and the NFL made one more small tweak that more than doubled the return rate. Rather than the 30-yard line, touchbacks into the endzone would now come out to the 35-yard line. In 2025, the NFL saw 8.0 returns per game, way up from 3.4 in the previous season, and a return rate of 75.9%.
